About Me

I'm currently a senior at Princeton University in the Department of Computer Science and the Programs of Entrepreneurship , Finance, and Technology and Society. I am passionate about the design of user experiences and the process of creating valuable technologies in our everyday lives.

Since 2015, I have been involved in Princeton's Engineers without Borders and have since led trips to Peru to build a gravity-fed water system for a community of 100 families in the Peruvian Andes. Now, as EWB President, I oversee the funding for our global development projects and direct our outreach initiatives.

In 2017, a few friends and I created the Company of Female Founders, where we use our passion for entrepreneurship to reach out to female leaders in the industry and empower other women on campus in their career and personal pursuits.

This past summer, I worked at United Parcel Services (UPS) as a UI/UX and Mobile Application Development Intern. In 2017, I was part of the Princeton Startup Immersion Program in Tel-Aviv, Israel. There, I worked at Systematic Inventive Thinking, Inc. (SIT) and completed the Venture Creation Course at the IDC Herzliya.

Engineers without Borders

The Princeton chapter of Engineers without Borders USA has been working on water systems in the countries of Peru, Kenya, and the Dominican Republic for the past 13 years. I have been involved in the Peru team since September 2015, where I was Head of Publicity and later, Project Manager. In these roles, I was in charge of managing a team of 40 students to design a water system, writing grants and documentation, and also leading a six-week trip to Peru to construct the water system. Currently, as President of chapter, I fundraise for all three teams, oversee the progress of all three projects, and manage our outreach and publicity efforts.
